Solar Water Treatment Methods

Solar Distillation

Solar distillation uses solar energy to convert saline or brackish water into potable water. The first large-scale solar distillation project was built in 1872 in Las Salinas, Chile, and could produce up to 22,700 L (6,000 US gal) of water per day.

Types of solar stills include: single-slope, double-slope, vertical, conical, inverted absorber, multi-wick, and multiple effect. The most economical for decentralized use is the double-slope still, while active multiple-effect units are better for large-scale applications.

Different Methods of Solar Water Treatment

Solar Distillation

Uses solar energy to distill water, producing clean water from saline or brackish sources. Examples of still types include double-slope and multiple effect.

Solar Water Disinfection (SODIS)

Exposes water-filled PET bottles to sunlight for several hours, which is recommended by the World Health Organization as a safe household method.

Solar Stabilization Ponds

Solar energy is used to treat wastewater in ponds, without chemicals or electricity. Algae grow in the ponds and help reduce carbon dioxide, though toxins may be produced.

Key Benefits and Applications

Additional Solar Water Treatment Methods

Solar Desalination

Solar desalination uses solar energy to remove salt and other impurities from seawater. It can be implemented using various technologies such as reverse osmosis powered by solar energy or multi-effect distillation.

Solar-Powered Desalination Units

Solar-powered desalination units are small-scale systems designed for off-grid locations. They are typically used in remote areas or islands where freshwater resources are scarce.

Historical Development

The first large-scale solar distillation project was constructed in 1872 in the Chilean mining town of Las Salinas, producing up to 22,700 L (6,000 US gal) of potable water daily.
